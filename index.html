The Big Picture — Why NextDimension Exists

Ever started importing huge photos into After Effects and watched your RAM get chewed up? A single 6000px photo from a DSLR uses far more memory than a 2500px version of the same image. Multiply that across dozens of stills in a composition and After Effects slows to a crawl — or crashes entirely.

NextDimension was built for exactly that moment. It lets you batch resize photos fast by custom width while keeping their natural aspect ratio. No uploads, no installs, no cloud processing. Everything runs 100% in your browser, so your photos never leave your device.

Built for After Effects & motion design workflows

  • Prep oversized stills before importing into After Effects
  • Reduce RAM pressure when working with photo-heavy compositions
  • Batch resize dozens of images to a consistent width in seconds
  • Keep natural proportions — no cropping, no stretching
  • Perfect for video editors, motion designers, and content creators

How it works

  • Drop your photos (JPG, PNG, WebP, BMP, TIFF)
  • Set your target width (e.g. 2500px for HD comps)
  • Choose output format and quality
  • Hit resize — files are saved directly to your chosen folder

Why resize instead of just scaling in After Effects?

After Effects loads every pixel of every image into RAM. A 6000×4000 photo takes up roughly 96 MB of uncompressed memory. Scale that down to 2500px wide and it drops to around 16 MB — six times less RAM per image. Across a 30-image slideshow, that is the difference between a smooth timeline and a project that stutters on every frame.

Privacy first

NextDimension processes everything locally using your browser's Canvas API. No files are uploaded anywhere. No analytics track your images. No account is needed. Your photos stay on your machine from start to finish.

Free, open, and lightweight

NextDimension is a single HTML file. No frameworks, no dependencies, no build tools. You can download it, bookmark it, or host it yourself. It is free to use for personal and commercial projects with no limits on file count or image size.
